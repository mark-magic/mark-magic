Madoka had been able to see the strings as long as she could remember, they were just always there, and her parents just thought she had a vibrant imagination, but she always knew it was a little more. Deep in her soul.

Everyone had them, stretching off and disappearing until they met the other side of that string. Of that relationship. Most were clear, nearly colorless, and as thin as thread. Countless bundles of them floating gently. They were so numerous that sometimes she wondered how people made that many connections. Met that many people.

The threads were also almost always paired, the two threads floating and stretching in a double helix, wrapping around each other. Not all were like that, but many.

The stronger ones though There were fewer, but they had colors, often many colors. Her own parents had quite a few purple ones, with other colors muddled through, in their threads of various sizes. Some were as thick as ribbons, while others were about as wide as hands. Her own two from her parents were large and beautiful, if a different shade of purple then her parents own intertwined ribbons, but just about as large.

Her friends though had much different ones, they had threads of faint purple going through them now, but they were mostly yellow, Sayakas leaning more towards a more golden color with white threaded through consistently, while Hitomis was more strongly yellow. Kyousukes were the faintest between them, but it wasnt by too much. Although he did have a weird one, that was fairly large and seemed to connect to his violin??? That was always an odd one.

The countless threads, strings, and ribbons They were beautiful. Criss crossing and becoming intertwined. She could always look, and understand the relationship between two people. Maybe not perfectly, but the ribbons didnt lie. No matter what color they were, they were never truly wrong. Whether they were green with rage, black with despair, or any other color, they never lied. Not even the grey, ripped and worn ones lied. Their remnants barely holding on to the barest remnants of what had been a connection.

Madoka still remembered beginning to understand what gray meant. Remember her parents each having one of their connections lose color, tear, and snap. It turned out after the fact that one of her grandparents had died.

She was still so thankful she told Hitomi and Sayaka the day after she found out, as the funeral was planned out. She had been scared, sad that someone she knew was dead, and hadnt been able to comprehend that she had known for over an hour before her Mom and Dad found out. Was thankful they had believed her, and seemed happy to ask her about the power.

She always wondered why she had them, and her friends had even helped with various possible answers for why she might, like her parents were superheroes or she just was the reincarnation of a magical girl and other things, but none ever felt quite like a purpose. None felt right. Like a way to use these powers to help people. The closest she had gotten was getting other people near her to help when there was a clearly unbalanced and scary pair of strings, like one between a bully and victim.

Then one day she woke up.

And found a massive, tightly coiled ribbon. It was a deep, bright purple, the size of her fist even with how twisted it was, and threaded through with other colors. Black, despair, and red, devotion were the most notable, but yellow friendship threads were also there in vast quantities, not to mention the occasional white hope thread and

The fading setting in across it, parts of it torn, other parts almost threadbare, shredded scraps barely holding on as the massive connection connected to her.

It was beautiful and terrifying in ways she didnt have words to describe. She had never even seen one this large before. She had only a handful of large connections herself, so it wasnt like she had much of an impact. It did seem possible for one person to know and care about someone in such a way. But this connection just hadnt been there last morning. Now it was so thick and obviously filled with an immense amount of emotion. She actually blushed a little at the thought that whoever was on the other end loved her, especially to this degree. Even if she wasnt fully sure whether it was platonic or romantic love. It was almost like one of those love letters she had been wanting to get.

The most terrifying part though, was the implication of the grey, torn parts.

Death.

By all rights, it should mean death.

Yet the connection wasnt snapped. It was still there, spitefully being held together, its own threads making sure even the most errant pieces didnt fly away in the wind.

She had no idea what this meant. And when she went to school that morning was even more confused when she saw a similar, but much smaller and more tattered ribbon connected to Sayaka. Its threads were green and yellow, other threads mixed through.

She thought about it for several days, wondering what it could mean as it twitched around, occasionally moving to practically her entire other side in the space between blinks. The only two things that came to mind was that it was the connection from some sort of ghost or guardian angel that she had just recently become able to see or well honestly she didnt even have a second explanation. She had no idea what it could be. What connection could just appear in such a state.

It was bizarre, and not normal.

Not that being able to see the strings that tie people together was normal. So she supposed she couldnt complain too much about things getting odder.

Although the dream she could barely remember, with a girl with long black hair fighting something immense, something white asking her for something asking her too

She couldnt remember.

When she went to school that day, she wasnt expecting anything. It was just a normal day at school, and she was starting to get used to the new ribbon, even if she didnt understand it.

Then as Saotome stopped her rant, a newly green and brown thread wearing away and smaller than it used to be floating about her, she welcomed a new transfer student.

Madokas eyes immediately widened in shock when the full Ribbon became visible. The massive ribbons owner suddenly got closer. Connecting together with hers and Sayakas, and even a faint and minuscule thread she hadnt noticed connecting to Hitomi.

She watched the beautiful girl step in front of class, and stare right at her after writing her name. Massive, almost ropelike ribbon tying them together.

And Madoka was only more confused.

Homura was still pretty slow at shuffling, which left her wondering why her new Why her new servant wasnt really good at such a small thing! Surely if she was as awesome as she seemed she would know how to do stuff like this right?

But Homura didnt seem to, so what was she bad at? What was she good at? She had to be amazing at something at least too! Even if whatever she was amazing at was also bad in some way that could lead to tragedy.

Something in Nagisa clicked at that thought as she watched Homura deal cards to them both for go fish, which Homura had needed to look up the rules for because she apparently didnt know them.

Homura kinda acted like a character from a tragedy.

She had some sort of issue with Kyubey, was really stoic, said mysterious things, was angry over her wasting her wish, did mysterious things, and was just weird in some ways. It made her wonder was Homura trying to atone for a tragedy or something? One she had been at the center of? One that probably involved magical girls?

Nagisa nodded to herself a little, that felt Right, even if she didnt know Homura very well yet.

But as they started playing, and she watched Homura awkwardly trying to take her turn, part of her felt that was kinda wrong too. At least unless Homura made the sort of wish that would like, cause a flame war from the arguments it caused. But

Well, it wasnt impossible.

Still, more importantly was that she had to figure out which cards to call out so she could win! If she didn't, Homura might beat her! And if she lost to someone who didnt seem to have ever played Go Fish before she was going to start a flame war of her own!

And hopefully by the time they finished, dinner would be done!
