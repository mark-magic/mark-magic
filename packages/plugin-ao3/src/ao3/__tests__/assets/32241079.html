Heartstrings

Summary:

Madoka had been able to see the strings as long as she could remember, they were just always there, and her parents just thought she had a vibrant imagination, but she always knew it was a little more. Deep in her soul.

Then one day, a new one appears out of nowhere. One she doesn't fully understand, and raises far too many questions.

Chapter 1

Chapter Text

Madoka had been able to see the strings as long as she could remember, they were just always there, and her parents just thought she had a vibrant imagination, but she always knew it was a little more. Deep in her soul.

 

Everyone had them, stretching off and disappearing until they met the other side of that string. Of that relationship. Most were clear, nearly colorless, and as thin as thread. Countless bundles of them floating gently. They were so numerous that sometimes she wondered how people made that many connections. Met that many people.

 

The threads were also almost always paired, the two threads floating and stretching in a double helix, wrapping around each other. Not all were like that, but many. 

 

The stronger ones though… There were fewer, but they had colors, often many colors. Her own parents had quite a few purple ones, with other colors muddled through, in their threads of various sizes. Some were as thick as ribbons, while others were about as wide as hands. Her own two from her parents were large and beautiful, if a different shade of purple then her parents own intertwined ribbons, but just about as large.

 

Her friends though had much different ones, they had threads of faint purple going through them now, but they were mostly yellow, Sayaka’s leaning more towards a more golden color with white threaded through consistently, while Hitomi’s was more strongly yellow. Kyousuke’s were the faintest between them, but it wasn’t by too much. Although he did have a weird one, that was fairly large and seemed to connect to his violin??? That was always an odd one.

 

The countless threads, strings, and ribbons… They were beautiful. Criss crossing and becoming intertwined. She could always look, and understand the relationship between two people.  Maybe not perfectly, but the ribbons didn’t lie. No matter what color they were, they were never truly wrong. Whether they were green with rage, black with despair, or any other color, they never lied. Not even the grey, ripped and worn ones lied. Their remnants barely holding on to the barest remnants of what had been a connection.

 

Madoka still remembered beginning to understand what gray meant. Remember her parents each having one of their connections lose color, tear, and snap. It turned out after the fact that one of her grandparents had died.

 

She was still so thankful she told Hitomi and Sayaka the day after she found out, as the funeral was planned out. She had been scared, sad that someone she knew was dead, and hadn’t been able to comprehend that she had known for over an hour before her Mom and Dad found out. Was thankful they had believed her, and seemed happy to ask her about the power.

 

She always wondered why she had them, and her friends had even helped with various possible answers for why she might, like her parents were superheroes or she just was the reincarnation of a magical girl and other things, but none ever felt quite like a purpose. None felt right. Like a way to use these powers to help people. The closest she had gotten was getting other people near her to help when there was a clearly unbalanced and scary pair of strings, like one between a bully and victim.

 

Then one day she woke up.

 

And found a massive, tightly coiled ribbon. It was a deep, bright purple, the size of her fist even with how twisted it was, and threaded through with other colors. Black, despair, and red, devotion were the most notable, but yellow friendship threads were also there in vast quantities, not to mention the occasional white hope thread and…

 

The fading setting in across it, parts of it torn, other parts almost threadbare, shredded scraps barely holding on as the massive connection connected to her.

 

It was beautiful and terrifying in ways she didn’t have words to describe. She had never even seen one this large before. She had only a handful of large connections herself, so it wasn’t like she had much of an impact. It did seem possible for one person to know and care about someone in such a way. But this connection just… hadn’t been there last morning. Now it was so thick and obviously filled with an immense amount of emotion. She actually blushed a little at the thought that whoever was on the other end loved her, especially to this degree. Even if she wasn’t fully sure whether it was platonic or… romantic love. It was almost like one of those love letters she had been wanting to get.

 

The most terrifying part though, was the implication of the grey, torn parts.

 

Death.

 

By all rights, it should mean death.

 

Yet the connection wasn’t snapped. It was still there, spitefully being held together, its own threads making sure even the most errant pieces didn’t fly away in the wind.

 

She had no idea what this meant. And when she went to school that morning was even more confused when she saw a similar, but much smaller and more tattered ribbon connected to Sayaka. Its threads were green and yellow, other threads mixed through.

 

She thought about it for several days, wondering what it could mean as it twitched around, occasionally moving to practically her entire other side in the space between blinks. The only two things that came to mind was that it was the connection from some sort of ghost or guardian angel that she had just recently become able to see or… well honestly she didn’t even have a second explanation. She had no idea what it could be. What connection could just… appear in such a state.

 

It was bizarre, and not normal.

 

Not that being able to see the strings that tie people together was normal. So she supposed she couldn’t complain too much about things getting odder.

 

Although the dream she could barely remember, with a girl with long black hair fighting something immense, something white asking her for something… asking her too…

 

She couldn’t remember.

 

When she went to school that day, she wasn’t expecting anything. It was just a normal day at school, and she was starting to get used to the new ribbon, even if she didn’t understand it.

 

Then as Saotome stopped her rant, a newly green and brown thread wearing away and smaller than it used to be floating about her, she welcomed a new transfer student.

 

Madoka’s eyes immediately widened in shock when the full Ribbon became visible. The massive ribbon’s owner suddenly got closer. Connecting together with hers and Sayaka’s, and even a faint and minuscule thread she hadn’t noticed connecting to Hitomi.

 

She watched the beautiful girl step in front of class, and stare right at her after writing her name. Massive, almost ropelike ribbon tying them together.

 

And Madoka was only more confused.

Madoka found her eyes wandering to Akemi as the class continued, the beautiful girl with a massive heartstring going to her of all people, and she still had no idea why. She’d never met the girl before besides in that odd dream. Yet the massive ribbon from the girl connecting them…

 

Love, despair, devotion, friendship, a little bit of hope, and death. All things she had no idea why a seemingly normal girl would have such a bond towards her. Knowing instinctively the greyness still meant death as the large almost ropelike bond almost twisted around her hand. A small and slowly forming thread of her own slowly returning the favor and wrapping around the larger ribbon.

 

But why did it mean death?

 

It wasn’t like such an effect could happen through a mistake. She knew that one particular one was rather literally what happened when one of the bondholders died. It only appeared then. She knew it innately. Deeply. In the same way she could see the bonds. 

 

So that meant something not normal was going on, like she had expected. But instead of it being some faceless guardian angel or ghost, the source was a transfer student in her class. She blushed lightly, looking away and letting go of the ribbon, although it didn’t let go of her. Staying wrapped around her wrist, and almost pulling on her. There wasn’t any real force but… She could feel it trying to pull her. She shivered, conflicted and confused emotions lancing through her.

 

Bonds would wrap around others a bit at times, but never felt like they were actually approaching exerting actual force.

 

Yet there was this one, which looked at all her knowledge of bonds and spat in its face.

 

That wasn’t even mentioning the other odd, semi shredded bonds coming out of the girl. Even if none were as large as her own, or purple with love...

 

Her thoughts continued to bounce between half formed idea’s as Saotome taught, wondering what was going on, and what to do about it. She couldn’t just pretend nothing was going on… could she?

 

By the time class ended, her thoughts were something a mess. The idea of Homura- Akemi being her guardian angel was incredibly distracting. She wasn’t sure what else it could be though.


Although on the other hand, she didn’t know what else it could be. Conceptually. She simply didn’t know what the limits of weirdness were. Madoka needed a reference point on what oddness there was in the world other than her power. But it was also the only one she knew.

 

Besides whatever was going on with Homura at least, as the girl was surrounded by other girls trying to find out about her. She wondered for a moment whether she should go over there herself to talk… but she wouldn’t be able to say anything in class. At least not without being labeled as crazy by everyone around her besides maybe Akemi. Just as she was about to try to think of something, the girl started holding her head, and said she needed to go to the nurse's office.

 

Madoka blinked, then remembered being told of the girl's condition, and immediately knew this was both her chance, and something she should help with. She prepared to get up, but Akemi beat her to it, and before she could even do anything, the girl had walked over to her.

 

“Could you escort me to the Nurse’s office?”

 

“Uh, of course!” Madoka exclaimed in slight shock. Although she supposed she shouldn't be given what her powers said.

 

During the walk though, she tried to talk to Akemi, no, the girl gave her permission to use her first name, Homura kept walking, giving her simple answers as she walked ahead of her, if she responded at all. At least until they reached the suspended pathway, and Homura turned towards her, asking her whether her current life, family, and friends were important to her.

 

Madoka’s eyes widened, wondering whether this was a ‘call to adventure’ even past her confusion at the abruptness of the question, not to mention the subject matter. After a moment of thought, she responded that she did. She loved them. When Homura asked her again to confirm, she was confused, but confirmed again.

Then Homura told her to never change or she would lose it all, that all she needed to be was Madoka Kaname, and then began walking away. The odd words leaving Madoka confused, especially in their earnestness.

 

As Madoka looked at the odd bond though, she immediately firmed her resolve.

 

“Wait!”

 

Homura stopped, looking back with a questioning expression, even as her lips stayed pressed together. “What is it?

 

Madoka almost hesitated, then forced herself to ask. “Are you… are you my guardian angel or something?”

 

She instantly felt stupid as soon as the words left her mouth. Flushing as the girl looked at her with emotions Madoka couldn’t quite parse flitting through her eyes.

 

Before she could even try to say she was joking or laugh it off or anything, Homura responded. “Why are you asking such a question?” 

 

Madoka blinked, as she wasn’t rejected out of hand, Homura seemed confused and shocked, to the point that she seemed… a lot more expressive than she had been before. Whatever facade she had put up cracking at the unexpected question.

 

She continued, hope and panic spurring her on. “I, um, I have had a special ability ever since I could remember… I can see threads and ribbons between people, representing their relationships and feelings about others. And a few days ago I woke up with a really big one out of nowhere attached to me. One that was weird in ways that shouldn’t be possible, and that I didn’t fully understand… Then when you entered the class I realized who the source was even if I didn’t understand… So I uh, well, with my ability I know there's odd magical things in the world, and due to the bond I can only assume there's something special about you too and that was one of the most obvious things that came to mind…”

 

Madoka flushed harder as she stopped herself from continuing to vomit out words. Especially given how Homura had gotten more shocked and confused the whole time she spoke. Had she messed things up between them trying to tell a virtual stranger about her power, even though her power showed her that Homura obviously cared for her for some reason? Even if something weird was obviously going on? She almost opened her mouth to try again to pass it off as a joke or something but… no words came out. She looked down, afraid that she’d messed up a friendship mounting, then soft hands came down on her shoulders, and she looked up into Homura’s shining purple eyes, eyes oh so reminiscent of the emotional bond tying Madoka to the black haired girl.

 

“I… I believe you. Meet me after school and we can talk.”

 

Homura turned quickly once more, prompting Madoka to quickly catch up as she walked, not entirely looking at her, but not looking away. Just still seeming confused and shocked, but not disbelieving, even as Madoka got into step next to the girl, who was obviously thinking deeply. Barely noticing the world around her.

 

Madoka looked at her own slowly coloring thread around the massive coiled ribbon though, and smiled softly as she saw something she hadn’t quite been expecting.

 

The large ribbon was slowly becoming whiter, the white threads starting to encroach on the black ones.

 

She had no idea what was happening with Homura.

 

But she was happy that hope was starting to bleach out and replace the girl's sadness and despair.

Homura was theirs now. Their friend. Their very, very, very hurt friend.

 

One of their best friends.

 

So she would figure out how to make sure that Homura felt welcome and happy.

Nagisa felt so hurt and just… she knew it was a bad idea to trust the animal, this wish would probably end like one of her tragedies if she made it. But at the same time, she wanted to show her mother just what sort of thing she could have done. How she could have saved her, but chose not to. Nagisa chose not to love her, just like she didn’t love Nagisa. 

 

So she was going to contract, and give her mom the cheesecake she loved instead of the life she wanted. Make her regret not loving Nagisa before… Well, she really wasn’t sure what would happen afterwards, after she let her Mom die. But she would probably be miserable, and she didn’t really care. 

 

As she stepped out of the hospital elevator though, following the animal out-who was that?

 

Nagisa blinked as she stared at the magical girl with black hair who was staring back with a blank expression.

 

The three stared for a moment, before Nagisa frowned. “Who are you?”

 

“I’m Homura Akemi, are you about to become a magical girl?”

 

She nodded. “I’m Nagisa, and yes, I want to. I have a wish I want to make, that I've thought long and hard about!” 

 

Homura frowned, but before she could say anything the weird animal spoke up telepathically. ”Homura Akemi has been talking to a lot of potential magical girls and trying to convince them to not make a wish. I don’t remember contracting her, and I suspect she has an ulterior motive . ”

 

The older girl glared at the animal. “How about I go and bring some of the magical girls I know for their opinions? You know Mami Tomoe and… Oriko Mikuni, right? How would you know what I was doing with the others anyways when you weren’t around, were you spying on us?”

 

Kyubey tilted its head slightly. “I do, but how do I know you haven’t gotten to them in some way?

 

Nagisa frowned, part of her wanting to just ignore the girl who was obviously going to try and tell her what to do. But at the same time… she seemed to know what being a magical girl meant, and part of her was very  interested in talking to someone the animal didn’t trust. For all that she didn’t plan to let this girl tell her what to do, she wasn’t about to let Kyubey tell her what to do either. “Why shouldn’t I make a wish?” She still had time anyways, her wish could wait a bit.

 

Homura seemed to be considering what to say for a moment, before slowly speaking up. “Firstly, becoming a magical girl is something you cannot take back. Once you make a wish, you have to fight forever until you either die or run out of magic. And… don’t run out of magic.” The girl visibly shivered a little at that, looking away from her as she did.

 

The animal tried to speak up, but Nagisa verbally plowed over it. “What if I don’t care? What if I want to wish for cheesecake anyways?”

 

She almost regretted saying that, Homura’s hands clenching into fists as the girl’s body language screamed suppressed rage. It almost made her want to go back to not being free, to not being a problem, to everything being Okay. But she absolutely refused to go back to that, even if it was hard.

 

Still, after a moment Homura’s voice came out steely and precise. “ Why  would you consider a da- a cheesecake worth fighting for the rest of your life?”

 

Nagisa didn’t cower even a little at that, even if it made her wonder if it really was that dumb. She… didn’t think it was. But at the same time, put that way, it was a lot harder to say she was confident in the idea. Even if she was also annoyed, and wanted to do it just because she was free now and nobody could tell her what to do. “I’m… I’m going to wish for the best cheesecake in the world, then give it to my Mom. I… I want her to know that I could have saved her, but wished for her favorite food instead! I want her to regret not loving me! To regret saying I ruined everything! That I should just kill everyone! I… I want to be happy!” Once she started, the rest just spilled out, emotions boiling over and leaving her shaking from their intensity. Her eyes slightly wet from the emotions that she instinctively tried to hold back and ignore. 

 

She didn’t want to be a good girl anymore, she would be a magical girl! But still…

 

Homura seemed shocked at her outburst, a mixture of emotions passing over her face for a moment eventually ending in exhaustion and then the blank mask again. “... I will admit, you have a better reason than… some others who considered that kind of wish. But if she’s really been that terrible to you, are you really going to waste your only chance to make any wish you want on someone who doesn’t care about you? Let her drag you down for the rest of your life like she seems to want? Or will you perhaps choose to live on and make a happy life of your own in spite of the love she decided not to give you?”

 

Nagisa blinked, part of her was angry and wanted to toss the words out of hand. To ignore the girl and make the wish she knew would be a bad decision, whether or not she had a selfish reason. But on the other hand, Homura had a point. It hurt, but… why was she letting her Mom drag her down like this…?

 

The answer was simple, and she hated it. She loved her mom. She loved her, and hated her, and wished things could go back to how they were. But it never would, even if she wished to cure her mom… She could feel herself shiver as she remembered her mom’s words. Her anger at everything and everyone. At her.

 

That happiness… part of her wanted it back. She desperately wanted the love she no longer had, wanted to do anything to get it back… make her mom regret not giving it to her. But… but even if she did, Dad was gone, and Mom would be soon too. Leaving her alone again, and fighting again for however long she could be a magical girl. Likely not a very long time either, she still doubted she would be very good at it.

 

So maybe this girl had a point… but she also didn’t have anyone. Didn’t really have a future she wanted to reach towards, or anyone to love her. If she didn’t… what did it matter?

 

Then suddenly, a thought hit her. Causing her to look up at what was likely a telepathic sparring match between the other two that she had been ignoring. “Homura, if I made a wish would you be my mentor?”

 

Homura seemed completely shocked at that, mouth actually open as the older girl just stared at her for a few moments. Seeming completely unsure what to say, after a second, the girl resolutely responded. “I… I will try, if you are so set on contracting, I will try.”

 

…

 

Nagisa nodded, and proceeded to walk back towards the elevator. Homura walked confusedly behind her a moment later as she felt Kyubey’s eyes on her back. It didn’t really matter.

 

Homura was willing to help her even when she didn’t get the result she wanted from Nagisa, and that was good enough. She wasn’t sure if this would still all end like a tragedy, or had made some mistake. Technically, she supposed, she was already making one by planning to follow a stranger. But at the same time, she didn’t care.

 

Her future didn’t exist with her Mom, and as much as it hurt… she only wanted to see her one more time.

 

“By the way, would you happen to know anywhere you can get really amazing cheesecake?”

 

Homura blinked a few times, before realization seemed to set in. She didn’t exactly smile, but she did close her eyes for a few moments. “... Yes, I think I know someone who would be willing to help you make a cheesecake.”

 

Nagisa smiled, it looked like she had her own little wish granter anyways! Who needed the animal!

Chapter 32

Chapter Text

Mami blinked as she heard a sudden knock at her door, putting down the bag full of school supplies as she turned back to the entrance. She wasn’t expecting anyone… not that she was usually expecting anyone. More importantly, with a quick pull of her magic she knew who it was, Akemi-senpai’s distinctive magic clear to her, soft yet rough like a desert in the night.

 

On the other hand was the other, whose potential was clear but vague. Unformed like Kaname’s or Miki’s, yet different from both. 

 

As she turned the doorknob, she wasn’t entirely sure what she was expecting. But when she saw… Well, the young girl at Akemi’s side made it rather clear why she was likely here.

 

“Well Akemi-senpai, it seems you have quite the habit don’t you~” She said with a perfect smile.

 

Akemi stared for a moment before her eyes widened, her expression immediately becoming blank again. “Not on purpose, I’m… honestly not sure how this happened twice.”

 

Her lips twitched for a moment as she held in laughter. “Perhaps it’s because you’re such a good senpai~ Although I’ve been terribly rude haven’t I? Hello there, I’m Mami Tomoe! Who are you?”

 

“Oh, I’m Nagisa Momoe. Are you the person who can help me make a cheesecake?”

 

Mami found herself processing that for a moment. “Ah, yes, I can do so. Why don’t you come in and we can start plans on it?”

 

Momoe nodded, smiling as she followed Akemi in. Now then, what did she have on hand again? She was pretty sure she had all the basic ingredients for cheesecake, so it would mostly be a matter of how involved Momoe wanted to be, and how she wanted it exactly. 

 

She went into the kitchen and prepared some tea, slowly waiting for the heat to be enough. Then going back out with a steaming pot of it, while she grabbed some chocolate cake with decorations covering it, only a single slice currently removed.

 

As she stepped into the main room, Momoe stared intently at the cake. Akemi simply raised an eyebrow at the younger girl, not that the white haired girl seemed to care, quickly eating through the slice she was given. While Akemi simply sipped her own tea.

 

Mami did the same, tasting the burning liquid and it's sweet flavor, it’s warmth spreading through her. 

 

“So, may I ask why you came to me for a cheesecake? I can do it of course, I’m just curious why you would want a more home baked one. Although I suppose they do tend to taste better.”

 

Momoe continued eating through her cake for a moment, before slowing down and looking up at her. “… I want to give one to my mom on my last visit before she gets too sick.”

 

Mami had to hold back a shiver, the situation suddenly becoming clearer. At least, somewhat. Part of her wanted to say it was just the girl wanting to do something kind for her seemingly dying mother, but something about the girl's tone was… odd. She turned to Homura slightly, reaching out with her mind. ”Am I… missing something?”

 

”... While I didn’t appreciate your words earlier, they were not wholly inaccurate. Her situation seems similar to some degree to Yuma’s. It is meant less as a gift, and more of a taunt.“

 

Ah, Mami took another sip of her tea to keep from staring at either of them. No longer able to taste the flavor past her thoughts and the part within her that bristled at the implied action. Another little girl with a mother like that… it almost made her wonder why she even protected this city.

 

Mami closed her eyes for a moment, remembering the countless good innocents who couldn’t defend themselves. Those she had succeeded, and those… those who she had failed.

 

She couldn’t fail them again.

 

Still, that led to another question. ”Why are you helping her with that then? At least in this way? I can’t imagine that buying a cheesecake wouldn’t also be a possibility.”

 

Homura didn’t react for a couple seconds. “While that would be the simple solution, I would prefer to not push the girl any further. She was intending to make a wish for that cheesecake, and was very headstrong about it. So if nothing else I would like to do this without her contracting.”

 

Ah, that… that would do it. A girl this young, contracting for such a reason… No, this was definitely a better course of action. Even if she wanted to make a contract anyways, it should be for herself, not for a simple cheesecake.

 

She forced herself to smile. “Alright then, what kind of cheesecake would you like made for her?” Perhaps it was in poor taste to help with something like this, but at the same time parents should care for their children. She knew how much she had loved her own, and to get her to the point of wanting to do something like this to them? It was unthinkable, cutting away any ounce of sympathy she might have had for the woman who was going to be ‘taunted’.

 

Momoe was thankfully very helpful there, bringing up the kind she ‘knew her mom liked’, nothing too difficult either. And when she asked if the younger girl wanted to join her in making it she smiled widely while nodding repeatedly. Following her back to the kitchen while Akemi continued working through her own refreshments and cake.

 

The cooking process itself wasn’t too difficult, with Momoe attempting to do whatever task she was asked even if she had to use a stool to reach high enough. The both of them worked together and slowly put the ingredients together with all the additions the girl called for until they put it in the oven. “Well then, besides some decorations and other minor things that should be it!”

 

“Good! I’ve never had to make something so big and with so many steps before! I want a break from baking!”

 

She laughed lightly, slowly moving them from the kitchen. “Well I assure you it will be delicious!”

 

Momoe just shrugged. “As long as it’s good enough for her, I honestly don’t really like cheesecake.”

 

She decided not to really touch on that whole… issue. Instead going for a different tack. “How about another slice of chocolate cake then?”

 

“Yes! Please! Thank you Tomoe-san!”

 

She laughed. “You’re welcome Momoe-san, although perhaps we should have dinner before any more sweets.”

 

The girl groaned, and Akemi’s lip twitched upwards slightly for a moment before nodding. “Agreed, we should have a decent dinner before any more desserts. Thank you for the reminder Tomoe.”

 

Well, clearly this had been quite the successful day! Now if only Kyo… Sakura was here, and things between them weren’t the way they were.

 

But it had only been a few months, and she knew things between them wouldn’t change. Not quickly at least.

Chapter 33

Chapter Text

The baking bit was hard and complicated, making her wonder if it was even worth it to not just use her wish. But now that they had finished it and she was snacking on some delicious chocolate cake she was feeling a lot happier about this! Mami made some really yummy cakes! 

 

Which also meant the cake currently being cooked would be really good too, as it needed to be! Homura had definitely made the right choice saying that Mami could do it, which really made sense if Baker Girl called Homura senpai! 

 

Nagisa was curious what Mami meant by Homura having a habit, did that mean she wasn’t the first girl the black haired girl had chosen to help? Or… something else? She didn’t know, and didn't care much either. Homura was nice to her so far, and that was what mattered. She could figure something out if things went wrong anyways! It wasn’t like she had really had anyone to help her for a while anyways, so even if things did go wrong… Admittedly, she didn’t know what would happen. She was pretty sure she had some family, but she didn’t know any of them very well, and she would probably be made to live with them unless she ran away?

 

Or maybe something else entirely would happen. Maybe she would simply be thrown away again.

 

She didn’t like that thought, so she bit down harder on her next chunk of delicious cake, loving the soft and sweet chocolate as she slowly ate more of it. Savoring it, even as she internally cursed Mami for saying they were going to have dinner before more dessert. She’d been living on her own for a while! She could decide for herself how much cake was too much!

 

Although the thought of having someone cooking her a nice full meal was… 

 

She wanted it. She wanted it a lot more than she had expected. She wanted a lot of things that felt like they were nearly crushing her chest with their sheer weight. Yet at the same time she… all she wanted was…

 

Nagisa couldn’t bring herself to word it, the thought hurt a lot even unworded, and she didn’t want more of that.

 

More importantly was that Mami was going off to make dinner! And she and Homura were left alone… and bored.

 

“Do you wanna play cards or something?” Mami probably had cards right? Or anything really, normally she just browsed the internet and stuff or read her books when she was bored, but it felt wrong doing those here and now. Not to mention she didn’t have her books.

 

Homura stared at her, gaze weirdly unwavering. But… it didn’t seem harsh? It was intense, and made her feel like Homura was staring into her soul, but it didn’t have the right sharpness for harshness or anger. So she stared right back, frowning as intensely as she could to show she was Serious.

 

After what felt like an eternity, Homura put an arm through her hair, flipping it. “Give me one moment to ask Mami Tomoe if she has any games for us to play.”

 

Nagisa smiled as Homura got up, so the girl was willing to be her mentor, help her get cheesecake, and play games with her? Just the thought of it left her cheeks feeling really stretched, a smile reflecting up at her from the glass table as she finished her chocolate cake.

 

After a minute, Homura came back with a basket which she put down next to the table, one filled with games and cards and stuff. Nagisa immediately grabbed for the cards, not really feeling up to learning a billion new rules to play one of the various board or card games. Not to mention Settlers of Catan didn’t sound all that interesting, even if she knew not to ‘judge a book by its cover’ that didn’t help the fact that it sounded boring.

 

Unfortunately it seemed neither of them were really good at shuffling, the cards being fumbled more than once until Homura got the hang of it… kinda. Homura was still pretty slow at shuffling, which left her wondering why her new… Why her new servant wasn’t really good at such a small thing! Surely if she was as awesome as she seemed she would know how to do stuff like this right?

 

But Homura didn’t seem to, so what was she bad at? What was she good at? She had to be amazing at something at least too! Even if whatever she was amazing at was also bad in some way that could lead to tragedy.

 

Something in Nagisa clicked at that thought as she watched Homura deal cards to them both for go fish, which Homura had needed to look up the rules for because she apparently didn’t know them.

 

Homura kinda acted like a character from a tragedy.

 

She had some sort of issue with Kyubey, was really stoic, said mysterious things, was angry over her ‘wasting’ her wish, did mysterious things, and was just weird in some ways. It made her wonder… was Homura trying to atone for a tragedy or something? One she had been at the center of? One that probably involved magical girls?

 

Nagisa nodded to herself a little, that felt Right, even if she didn’t know Homura very well yet. 

 

But as they started playing, and she watched Homura awkwardly trying to take her turn, part of her felt that was kinda wrong too. At least unless Homura made the sort of wish that would like, cause a flame war from the arguments it caused. But…

 

… Well, it wasn’t impossible.

 

Still, more importantly was that she had to figure out which cards to call out so she could win! If she didn't, Homura might beat her! And if she lost to someone who didn’t seem to have ever played Go Fish before she was going to start a flame war of her own!

 

And hopefully by the time they finished, dinner would be done!
